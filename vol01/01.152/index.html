The Thousand Kingdoms

Long ago, magic began to fade. In an act of desperation it was sealed away. But now those seals are failing and magic returns to the world. Ella, a physics grad student in NYC, is thrown into the deep end and must find a way to reconcile the modern and the magical.

428 words Previous post: Next post:

Ella was woken abruptly by Zaidu, she had fallen asleep in her gear, her mask comfortable against her face.

It was night and the moonlight lit the fire-strewn field from the quick encampment they had built. They were near the castle now. She could almost hear the pennants.

And across that field stood the Emperor and orcs flanking him. These orcs were better armed, looked harder. Elites.

Ella stood up, despite the hiss from Zaidu, she could feel her crown settle on her head and that ethereal connection to the crown of the Emperor. it felt clearer now that they were closer, she could feel the tug of war in power.

Her face smiled behind the mask and she gave a strong “pull” on the tether and watched the crown on the Emperor dim. did his eyes narrow in surprise? She couldn’t tell across the field, but she thought so.

They could have stared at one another daring the other to speak as if such meaningless political games meant anything here, but Ella knew they did not.

“My Emperor,” her voice was hoarse from smoke and yelling on the battlefield. Her tone was mocking in its supposed sincerity. She swept a curtsy, looking ridiculous in torn and bloody tactical gear.

The Emperor thought they were playing the game and I coined his head and nodded as if he was receiving his due.

He spoke quietly but the waves of sound were subtly sustained, reverberating through the air without decay.

“Our wayward princess returns. We welcome the return of our Dagger and hope to save you from yourself.”

“Why would I need saving?”

The Emperor stood across from her, regarding her. His eyes glowed in the distance much like hers. His stance was proud, his posture straight, so different from that time not so long ago in the throne room.

“We see you do not know. Have you stumbled onto both Appropriation and Investituture unknowingly?”

He began to laugh in a way that didn’t match the formality of his previous speech.

“Our young foolish princess. You have been partaking in the blessing of the Aqrabuamelu, eating the dead and taking their strength, their vitality… and their cruelty.” He was stumbling through the laughter to get the words out. it grated on her.

“And to compound it further, you took that cruelty, that anger, that coarsening of morality, and began to Invest it into that mask you wear. It is nascent and when you wear it you will be powerful but far, far more vicious than I have ever been.”
