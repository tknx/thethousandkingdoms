The People

2021-11-24
3 min read

Ascended - Ascended are a category for beings beyond the terrestrial plane. They may be individuals who met an unknown threshold and now exist on higher planes of existence. Or native species from those planes. When they manifest on the terrestrial plane, the inhabitants will perceive bizarre sensory aberrations because of the Ascended’s partial manifestation and power resonance from the higher plane.

Aqrabuamelu - The Aqrabuamelu are the Akkadian-mythological scorpion people from thee Epic of Gligamesh. Tiamat created them to war against the other gods. In this story, they were created by Ekerri and programmed with religious adoration to him as the deity, Utu, and given false prophecies to control them through hope of eventual salvation. They typically look Middle Eastern with a slight reddish cast to their skin, and can innately transform between human and half-human/half-scorpion shapes. Lost arts include transforming into a full scorpion, eating your enemies and gaining strength, and true reproduction (they use the waning magic in the world to reproduce in smaller and smaller numbers)

Dragons - Ancient and powerful, these shape-shifting creatures are innately magical and thus survived the sequestering of magic by Ekerri. They were some of Ekerri’s most powerful foes. They are natives if the ascendant planes and are now inhabitants on both terrestrial and ascended planes.

Drow - Are an earlier experiment of Ekerri’s to breed spies and assassins. Because they were meant to run long missions alone, they were imprinted with an absolute fanaticism to Ekerri as their God but it made them inflexible and unimaginative.

Dwarves - Dwarf are 120 to 140cm (4’ to 4'6) and appear to be roughly chiseled out of stone and rock. Their clans are organized by rough colorations although intermarriage is common. They are agendered and reproduce through some form of recombinative technique that reconstitutes the parents into multiple offspring. They do mate for their lives and will not take another mate.

Elves - Elves often fall within the “uncanny valley” of being different. Take a human and stretch them vertically. Their cheekbones are very sharp and they have elongated chins giving them triangular faces. Their eyes are canted an uncomfortable looking 30-35 degrees.

Fae - Fae will typically follow idealized human norms in sizing and appearance, but with metallic skin (gold for summer, silver for winter) and wings. They are fluid in gender and identity, the same fae shifting between both male, female, non-sexual, and hermaphroditic forms; as well as taking on new names that do not often coincide with appearance changes. They will change affiliation between courts in internecine struggles and their skin will change suit. Their wings will ape a variety of normal species such as birds and butterflies and do not shift as the fae changes shape. It is unclear what meaning the wings may have. The fae are notorious for lying through omission, but their social norms are rigorously enforced by other fae.

Nüwa - Nüwa are part dragon and part either human or elf who wildly vary in how much of their body presents as reptilian and how mach as human or elf based on early mosaicization and differentiation. So a Nüwa may be almost entirely dragon or entirely human or elf; or some midpoint between.

Orc - Orcs range from 190cm to 200cm with less variation than humans. Their skin color will change from dusky grey-green to dark grey with age. Eyes typically resemble a cats ranging in color from yellow to green. Orcs lack a nose or ears, instead having holes set in their head. Their teeth are carnivorous and like sharks, they will often lose teeth consistently and regrow them. An orc will typically be 20-30% stronger than an equivalently sized human.
