The Thousand Kingdoms

Long ago, magic began to fade. In an act of desperation it was sealed away. But now those seals are failing and magic returns to the world. Ella, a physics grad student in NYC, is thrown into the deep end and must find a way to reconcile the modern and the magical.

01.010 Prep

175 words

Dealing with one’s parents is often something that is brushed over in fantasy. You can’t have adventures with watchful parents. I somewhat get around this by having my protagonist be older, in grad school. But that doesn’t mean she is estranged from her family, just exasperated sometimes.

Her father being an actor once and now a professor and her mother being a physician is intentional. She comes from an educated background but not one that is solely that way. Having a much younger brother, in high school, let’s me have it both ways a bit.

You can also see her prepping as she can. She’s been stabbed twice and lives in New York and isn’t a gangster. So she isn’t going to be going all Commando and carrying around weapons. But picking up pepper spray? Thinking about how to become stronger and more prepared given the constraints of real life? That makes sense.

She is also starting to practice at least manifesting what magic she has. She has no idea what good it is yet.
